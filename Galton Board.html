Why a bell curve? Each ball bounces left or right randomly at every peg — like flipping a coin. Most balls accumulate near the center (roughly equal lefts & rights). Ending far out requires an unlikely streak of the same outcome — so the edges stay thin. Many small random steps always produce a normal distribution — this is the Central Limit Theorem.
