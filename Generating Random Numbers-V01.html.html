Mathematical Insight
U ~ Uniform(0,1)
Start with a uniform random variable. Every value in [0,1] is equally likely — this is what computers generate natively.
X = F⁻¹(U)
Apply the inverse CDF. The steep regions of F attract more samples → high probability density there.
F_X(X) ~ Uniform(0,1)
The CDF of X evaluated at X is always uniform. This symmetry is the mathematical heart of the method.
PROOF SKETCH
P[Y ≤ y]
= P[F⁻¹(U) ≤ y]
= P[U ≤ F(y)]
= F(y)  ✓
