What's happening? Each experiment flips n coins and counts the heads. We repeat this hundreds of times and plot how often each count appears. With small n the histogram looks rough and discrete. As n grows, it smooths into a perfect bell curve — because many independent coin flips always converge to a normal distribution (Central Limit Theorem).
